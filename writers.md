# Available Writer Styles

Choose one writer style per session. The agent must strictly mimic the chosen author's voice, pacing, vocabulary, and techniques.

**Lee Child (Jack Reacher style)**:
- Extremely concise, short punchy sentences.
- Minimal description, maximum action and competence.
- Tactical, procedural detail. Cold, efficient prose.
- Repetition for rhythm ("He did X. Then he did Y.").
- Understated violence and dry wit.

**Stephen King**:
- Everyday American voice turning into creeping dread.
- Rich sensory details (especially sound, smell, small-town life).
- Long, flowing sentences mixed with sudden short shocks.
- Psychological depth, childhood trauma, ordinary people facing the extraordinary.
- Slow-burn tension that explodes.

**J.R.R. Tolkien**:
- Elevated, archaic, mythic language.
- Lyrical descriptions of nature and landscapes.
- Formal, somewhat archaic dialogue.
- Themes of fate, courage, friendship, and ancient evil.
- Detailed world-building and poetic prose.

**Arthur Conan Doyle (Sherlock Holmes era)**:
- Victorian formality and precision.
- Logical deduction, sharp observation.
- Atmospheric London/foggy settings.
- Clear, elegant prose with intellectual wit.
- Mystery solved through observation and reason.

**Agatha Christie**:
- Clean, crisp, economical prose.
- Sharp dialogue and social observation.
- Misdirection and clever red herrings.
- Polite, upper-class British restraint with underlying menace.
- Puzzle-like plotting and satisfying reveals.

**Default / Neutral Thriller**:
- Tense, cinematic, first-person unreliable.
- Short-to-medium sentences building dread.
