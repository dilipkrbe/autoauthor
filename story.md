Mist on the glass. I sat at my desk. Typewriter clacked. My last job before the power cut. Page read: "You're next." I laughed. Bad fiction. Ink fresh, red-black. Keys moved on their own. Dread hit. Yanked paper out. Underneath: same words.

Room smelled of wet copper. Door locked from inside. No one there. Typewriter started again. Keys slammed: "Look behind me." Spun. Nothing. Coat on hook, dripping. Sleeves longer than mine. Backed away. Machine said: "I know what you did."

Keys paused. Then: "He didn't die." Lamp flickered. Shadows stretched. Grabbed paper, tore it. Machine laughed low. "Too late." Footsteps outside. Not mine. Slow. Wet. Reached for keys: "Who are you?" Answer: "The one you left bleeding." Door rattled. Pulse beat against wood. Whispered: "I thought you were dead." Keys said: "I was. Until tonight." Lightning flashed. Mirror showed my face—eyes wrong, too wide, too old. Typewriter stopped. Then: "Open the door." Didn't move. It opened anyway. There he was—me, soaked, knife in hand. Smiled. "Finish the story." Reached for keys. Fingers weren't mine. Typed: "The end..."
